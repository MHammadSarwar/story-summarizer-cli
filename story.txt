On a dry summer day, a little sparrow flew across a barren valley searching for food. 
All the fields were empty, and her stomach rumbled with hunger. She searched for hours 
until she finally spotted a large, ripe sunflower growing near an abandoned farmhouse.
Excitedly, the sparrow flew down, but she quickly realized the heavy seeds were locked 
tight inside the tough, fibrous center of the flower. She pecked at it with all her 
strength, but her tiny beak could not break the surface. Instead of quitting, she noticed
a family of industrious ants working near the roots of the plant.She chirped softly to the
ants and offered a deal, proposing to shake the tall stalk so the loose seeds at the top would 
fall down to the ground for everyone to share. The ants agreed and began gnawing at the base of 
the stem to loosen it, while the sparrow used her wings to flap hard against the flower head.
Together, their combined efforts caused the sunflower to bend, releasing a shower of nutritious 
seeds. Both the sparrow and the ants ate until they were completely full, surviving the harsh 
season because they chose to cooperate.This simple story teaches us that teamwork and mutual help 
can solve difficult problems that we cannot face alone.
